# Business Requirements Document (BRD): DECP Platform

---

## 1. Executive Summary

* **Business Problem:** University departments suffer from fragmented communication and a weak link between academic experience and professional opportunity. Students have no centralised way to discover job openings shared by alumni, attend departmental events, find research collaborators, or build a professional network rooted in their academic community. Alumni have no structured channel to give back - whether by posting roles, mentoring students, or staying engaged with current research. Faculty have no unified tool to announce projects, recruit student collaborators, or broadcast events. Existing general-purpose social networks (LinkedIn, Facebook) do not cater to the specific, trust-based context of a university department.

* **Proposed Solution:** DECP (Department Engagement & Career Platform) is a purpose-built, role-aware social and career platform for university departments. It consolidates professional networking, job and internship advertising, event management, academic research collaboration, and direct communication into a single, authenticated environment - accessible via both a web browser and a mobile application - allowing students, alumni, and faculty to engage meaningfully within their shared academic community.

---

## 2. Business Objectives & Goals

* **Objective 1: Strengthen Alumni–Student Career Pathways.** Enable alumni to post verified job and internship opportunities directly to the department community, and enable students to discover, apply for, and track those opportunities - creating a trusted, department-curated career marketplace that reduces reliance on external, unverified job boards.

* **Objective 2: Increase Departmental Engagement and Retention.** Provide a living social feed, events calendar, and notification system to keep all members - current students, graduates, and staff - continuously engaged with department activities. Reduce the information gap that causes alumni to disengage from their academic community after graduation.

* **Objective 3: Facilitate Academic Research Collaboration.** Give faculty and students a structured workspace to advertise research projects, recruit collaborators across cohorts, share documents, and track project progress - increasing the volume and visibility of departmental research output.

* **Objective 4: Provide Data-Driven Operational Oversight.** Equip department administrators with an analytics dashboard that surfaces platform usage trends, engagement metrics, and content performance - enabling evidence-based decisions on departmental programmes and resource allocation.

* **Objective 5: Ensure a Secure, Trustworthy Community Environment.** Because the platform deals with sensitive academic and career data (resumes, research documents, personal profiles), the system must enforce strict identity verification, role-based access controls, and content ownership rules to maintain community trust.

## 3. Project Scope

### In Scope
The following business capabilities are fully implemented in the delivered codebase:

* **Identity & Access Management:** User registration, email-based identity verification, credential login, Google social login, password reset, session management via rotating JWT tokens, and role-based permissions.
* **Professional Profiles:** Rich, LinkedIn-style personal profiles including work experience, education history, skills, certifications, social links, and an "open to work" status flag.
* **Social Networking:** A follower/connection graph allowing members to build their professional network within the department.
* **Social Content Feed:** Creation and consumption of text, image, video, document, and poll posts with full social interaction (likes, comments, shares, bookmarks).
* **Jobs & Internships Board:** End-to-end job lifecycle from posting (alumni/faculty/admin only) through application (with resume upload and cover letter) to application status tracking.
* **Events & Announcements Calendar:** Creation and management of departmental events (webinars, workshops, career fairs, etc.) with RSVP functionality and capacity enforcement.
* **Research Project Collaboration:** Project management workspace with document upload, collaborator management, progress tracking, and visibility controls.
* **Direct & Group Messaging:** Real-time one-on-one and group conversations with file/image attachment support.
* **Notifications Centre:** In-app and mobile push notification delivery for all significant platform events.
* **Analytics Dashboard:** Platform-wide activity metrics, content performance reporting, and engagement trend analysis for administrators.
* **Mobile Application:** A companion React Native (Expo) mobile app mirroring the core platform capabilities.
* **Administration Controls:** User deletion and platform-wide analytics oversight for admin-role users.

### Out of Scope
The following capabilities are **not present** in the codebase and were not delivered:

* **Payment Processing:** No fee collection, subscription billing, or payment gateway integration is implemented. Any monetisation model would need to be built.
* **Video Conferencing / Webinar Hosting:** Events can store a virtual meeting link (e.g., a Zoom or Google Meet URL), but no native video call infrastructure is built into the platform.
* **Content Moderation Tools:** No automated content flagging, reporting mechanism, or moderator review queue is present. Admins can delete users, but there is no explicit post-reporting or abuse-management workflow.
* **Alumni Verification / Credential Verification:** Users self-declare their role (student, alumni, faculty) at registration. No integration with a student information system (SIS) or institutional identity provider to verify claimed roles exists.
* **Mentorship Matching Engine:** While the platform enables connections and messaging, there is no structured mentorship pairing or matching algorithm.
* **Group / Community Spaces:** Posts and content are per-user on a single shared feed; there are no sub-groups, departments-within-departments, or topic-based channels beyond research projects.

## 4. Stakeholders & Users

* **Student:** Joins the platform to build a professional profile, discover and apply for jobs and internships posted by alumni, attend departmental events, join research projects as a collaborator, and connect with peers and alumni for networking and mentorship. The default user role assigned at registration.

* **Alumni:** Uses the platform to stay engaged with their department community after graduation, post job and internship opportunities at their organisations, mentor students, participate in research projects, and attend or organise events. Distinguished from students by the `alumni` role and optional graduation year.

* **Faculty:** Uses the platform to create and lead research projects, recruit student and alumni collaborators, organise departmental events (seminars, workshops, webinars), and communicate through the social feed and messaging system.

* **System Administrator (Admin):** Responsible for platform health and governance. Accesses the analytics dashboard to monitor platform usage and engagement, manages user accounts (including deactivation and deletion), and can override content ownership controls (e.g., delete any job or event regardless of who posted it).

## 5. Business Rules & Logic

The following rules are enforced by the system's validation schemas, database constraints, and permission checks:

**Identity & Access**

* **Rule 1 — Unique Email Registration:** Every account must be associated with a unique email address. An attempt to register with an already-used email address is rejected with a conflict error. *(Enforced by `UNIQUE` constraint on the `users.email` column.)*

* **Rule 2 — Password Minimum Length:** Passwords must be between 6 and 128 characters. This rule applies to both initial registration and the password-reset flow. *(Enforced by the `registerSchema` and `resetPasswordSchema` Joi validators.)*

* **Rule 3 — Email Verification Required for Full Participation:** Upon registration, a user's `isEmailVerified` flag is set to `false`. A verification email containing a one-time token is sent automatically. Users authenticated via Google OAuth bypass this step and are marked as verified immediately. *(Enforced by the `emailVerificationToken` flow in the Auth Service.)*

* **Rule 4 — Session Tokens Expire:** Access tokens are short-lived (15 minutes by default). Long-lived refresh tokens expire after 7 days. A client must obtain a new access token using the refresh token before the access token expires; re-authentication is required after the refresh token expires. *(Enforced by JWT `expiresIn` configuration.)*

* **Rule 5 — Sensitive Operations Are Rate-Limited:** Brute-force attacks on authentication and password reset endpoints are mitigated by a strict rate limit of 5 requests per 15-minute window per IP address. General API usage is subject to a broader rate limit. *(Enforced by `strictRateLimiter` and `rateLimiter` middleware on the API Gateway.)*

**Jobs & Careers**

* **Rule 6 — Only Authorised Roles May Post Jobs:** Only users with the `alumni`, `faculty`, or `admin` role may create job or internship listings. Students are explicitly blocked from creating job posts. *(Enforced by role check in `createJob` controller.)*

* **Rule 7 — One Application Per Job Per User:** A user can only submit one application to any given job listing. Duplicate application attempts are rejected. *(Enforced by a composite `UNIQUE` database index on `[jobId, userId]` in the `applications` table.)*

* **Rule 8 — Only the Job Poster May Manage Their Own Listings:** A job listing can only be edited by the user who created it. Deletion is permitted to either the original poster or an admin. *(Enforced by ownership check `job.postedBy !== userId` in `updateJob` and `deleteJob` controllers.)*

* **Rule 9 — Cover Letters Are Limited to 5,000 Characters:** Application cover letter text is capped at 5,000 characters. *(Enforced by `createApplicationSchema` Joi validator.)*

**Events**

* **Rule 10 — Events Enforce Attendance Capacity:** If an event organiser sets a capacity, the RSVP system blocks additional "going" RSVPs once that capacity is reached, returning a "Event is at capacity" error. *(Enforced by RSVP count check in `rsvpEvent` controller.)*

* **Rule 11 — Only the Event Organiser May Modify Their Event:** An event's details can only be modified by the user who created it. Deletion is permitted to either the organiser or an admin. *(Enforced by ownership check `event.organizerId !== userId`.)*

* **Rule 12 — RSVP Statuses Are Constrained:** An RSVP response must be one of three values: `going`, `maybe`, or `not_going`. *(Enforced by `rsvpSchema` Joi validator.)*

**Research Collaboration**

* **Rule 13 — Research Project Access Is Controlled by Visibility:** Research projects can be set to `public` (visible to all), `department` (visible within the department community — the default), or `private` (visible only to the lead and collaborators). *(Enforced by visibility filter in `getProjects` query.)*

* **Rule 14 — Only Project Members May Modify Research Projects:** Editing a research project's details or uploading documents requires the user to be either the designated lead researcher or a listed collaborator. Non-members receive a 403 Forbidden response. *(Enforced by lead/collaborator membership check in `updateProject` and `addDocument` controllers.)*

* **Rule 15 — Research Project Progress Is Bounded:** The progress field for a research project must be an integer between 0 and 100 (representing a percentage). *(Enforced by `Joi.number().integer().min(0).max(100)` in `projectSchema`.)*

**Content & Social Feed**

* **Rule 16 — Post Content Is Capped at 5,000 Characters:** The body of any social post is limited to 5,000 characters. *(Enforced by `createPostSchema` Joi validator.)*

* **Rule 17 — Comments Must Be Non-Empty and Capped:** Comments must contain at least 1 character and cannot exceed 2,000 characters. *(Enforced by `createCommentSchema`.)*

* **Rule 18 — Uploaded File Types Are Strictly Validated:** File uploads (post media, research documents, resumes) are validated against a whitelist of permitted MIME types (JPEG, PNG, GIF, WebP, MP4, WebM, OGG, PDF). Critically, validation is performed by inspecting the file's actual binary magic bytes, not just its declared MIME type or file extension, to prevent file-type spoofing attacks. *(Enforced by `validateAndSaveFiles` middleware using `SIGNATURES` magic-byte map.)*

* **Rule 19 — Maximum 5 Media Files Per Post:** A single post can carry a maximum of 5 attached media files. *(Enforced by `memoryUpload.array('media', 5)` Multer limit.)*

* **Rule 20 — File Size Limit of 20 MB Per Upload:** Individual uploaded files cannot exceed 20 MB. *(Enforced by `limits: { fileSize: 20971520 }` in Multer configuration.)*

**Networking**

* **Rule 21 — Users Cannot Follow Themselves:** A user attempting to create a connection with their own account ID is rejected. *(Enforced by `followerId === followingId` check in `followUser` controller.)*

* **Rule 22 — Duplicate Connections Are Prevented:** A user cannot send a second connection request to the same person. *(Enforced by composite `UNIQUE` index on `[followerId, followingId]` in the `connections` table.)*

## 6. High-Level Process Flows

### Process A: New Member Onboarding

1. A new user navigates to the platform and selects "Register."
2. They provide their email address, full name, a password (minimum 6 characters), and select their role (Student, Alumni, or Faculty), along with their department and graduation year where applicable.
3. The system creates their account, sends a verification email to the provided address, and returns a session token — granting immediate (but unverified) platform access.
4. The user clicks the verification link in the email, confirming their identity and marking their account as verified.
5. The user's profile is auto-created and they are taken to the main social feed to begin engaging with the community.

### Process B: Job Application Lifecycle

1. An alumni or faculty member creates a job or internship listing, specifying the role title, company, location, employment type, required skills, and an optional salary range and expiry date.
2. The listing is published with `active` status and becomes discoverable to all authenticated platform members via the Jobs Board, with search and filter capabilities.
3. A student (or alumni) reviews a listing and submits an application, uploading their resume and an optional cover letter (up to 5,000 characters).
4. The system enforces that the applicant has not already applied for the same listing (one application per user per job).
5. The job poster reviews received applications. They advance the application through status stages: `pending` → `reviewing` → `interview` → `offered` or `rejected`.
6. The applicant can monitor the real-time status of all their applications from their personal "My Applications" view.
7. When the role is filled or the listing expires, the poster sets the job status to `closed`, removing it from the active listings.

### Process C: Research Collaboration

1. A faculty member (or other authorised user) creates a research project, providing a title, abstract, field of research, intended timeline, and sets its visibility (public, department, or private).
2. The project appears on the Research page, discoverable by community members according to its visibility setting.
3. Interested students or alumni request to collaborate (`POST /research/{id}/collaborate`), joining the project's collaborators list.
4. All project members (lead and collaborators) can upload research documents (PDFs and supported file types) to the project's document store.
5. The lead researcher tracks and updates the project's completion progress (0–100%) as work advances through `planning` → `active` → `completed` phases.
6. Completed projects remain visible on the platform as a record of the department's research output.

### Process D: Event Discovery and Attendance

1. An organiser (any authenticated member) creates an event, selecting its type (webinar, workshop, seminar, career fair, etc.), date, and whether it is physical or virtual (with a meeting link). An optional attendance capacity can be set.
2. The event, once published, appears on the Events Calendar and is discoverable by all community members.
3. Community members RSVP to events they wish to attend (`going`, `maybe`). If the event has a capacity limit, the system rejects RSVPs that would exceed it.
4. Attendees can view their upcoming events via the "My RSVPs" filter.
5. The organiser can update event details or cancel the event, changing its status accordingly.

### Process E: Administrative Oversight

1. An admin logs in and navigates to the Analytics Dashboard.
2. The dashboard presents platform-wide metrics: total registered users, total posts, active job listings, and user activity trends.
3. The admin selects a time period (week, month, year) to view time-series activity charts, content engagement breakdowns, and popular content reports.
4. If a user account requires action (e.g., policy violation), the admin navigates to user management and can deactivate or delete the account.
5. The admin can also override content ownership to delete any job listing or event regardless of who created it.

## 7. Financial & Market Context (Human Input Required)

* **Target Market:** Platform is intended to be licensed to multiple universities as a SaaS product.

* **Deployment Model:** A single-tenant deployment per institution or a multi-tenant SaaS product.

* **Expected ROI / Success Metrics:** Number of student–job matches per semester, alumni re-engagement rate, number of active research projects, event attendance rates and monthly active users.

* **Monetisation Strategy:** The platform currently has no payment or subscription infrastructure. Future plans include premium features, corporate job-posting fees, or white-label licensing to other institutions.
