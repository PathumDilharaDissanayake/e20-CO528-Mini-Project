## 1. Welcome & Getting Started

### 1.1 What Is the DECP Platform?

The **Department Engagement & Career Platform (DECP)** is an online community designed exclusively for the University of Peradeniya's academic community. Think of it as a private social and professional network — like LinkedIn, but built specifically for your department. It brings together students, alumni, and faculty in one place where you can:

- Share updates and ideas with your department community
- Search for internships, jobs, and research opportunities
- Attend or host workshops, webinars, and career events
- Collaborate on academic research projects
- Message fellow members directly or in group chats
- Build a professional academic profile to showcase your skills and experience

---

### 1.2 Who Can Use It?

The platform has three member types. Your role determines which features you can access:

| Role | Who | What they can do |
|------|-----|-----------------|
| **Student** | Currently enrolled students | Browse and apply for jobs; attend events; join research projects; post and connect |
| **Alumni** | Graduated members | Everything students can do, **plus** post job listings |
| **Faculty** | Academic staff | Everything alumni can do, **plus** create events and start research projects |
| **Admin** | Platform administrator | Full access to all features and the Admin Analytics dashboard |

> **Note:** You select your role when you register. The "Admin" role cannot be self-selected — it is assigned by the platform administrator.

### 1.3 Creating Your Account

Registration takes about two minutes and is split into three easy steps.

Fill in the following fields:

| Field | What to Enter |
|-------|---------------|

### 1.4 Signing In to Your Account

![alt text](image.png)

1. Go to the login page (you will be taken here automatically if you are not signed in).
2. Enter your **Email Address**.
3. Enter your **Password**. Use the eye icon on the right side of the field to show or hide what you are typing.
4. Click **Sign In**.

On success, you will be taken directly to your Feed.

**Alternative sign-in:** You may also use the **Sign in with Google** button to log in using your Google account.

If you signed in from a link to a specific page, you will be redirected to that page after logging in.

---

### 1.5 Forgot Your Password?

1. On the login page, click the **Forgot Password?** link beneath the Sign In button.
2. Enter the **Email Address** associated with your account.
3. Click **Send Reset Link**.
4. Check your email inbox. You will receive a message with a link to reset your password.

After submitting, the page displays: *"Check Your Email — We've sent a password reset link to your email address. Please check your inbox and follow the instructions."*

A green confirmation notification will also appear briefly at the top of the screen.

## 3. Core Workflows

### 3.1 Your Feed — Sharing & Engaging with Posts

The Feed is the first thing you see after logging in. It shows a stream of posts from members across your department.

[INSERT SCREENSHOT HERE — Feed page with CreatePost box at the top and a post below]

#### Creating a New Post

The **Create Post** box sits at the very top of your feed.

1. Click inside the text area and type what you want to share (up to **5,000 characters**).
2. A character counter in the corner shows how many characters you have used.

**Attaching photos or videos:**

- Click the **photo icon** (🖼) in the toolbar to select images (JPEG, PNG, GIF, or WebP).
- Click the **video icon** (🎬) to attach a video (MP4, WebM, or OGG).
- You can attach up to **5 files** at a time. Each file must be **20 MB or smaller**.
- Selected files appear as thumbnails below the text box. Click the **×** on a thumbnail to remove it.

**Creating a Poll:**

1. Click the **poll icon** (📊) in the toolbar. A poll builder will appear below the text box.
2. Enter at least **2 poll options** (up to a maximum of 6). Each option is a short text field.
3. Type your question or context in the main text area.
4. Click **Post** to publish.

**Publishing your post:**

Click the **Post** button. You will see a notification confirming: *"Post created successfully!"* (or *"Poll created successfully!"* for polls).

[INSERT SCREENSHOT HERE — CreatePost box with toolbar buttons visible and a sample poll]

#### Interacting with Posts

Each post in your feed shows action buttons along the bottom:

| Button | What it does |
|--------|-------------|
| ❤️ **Like** | Tap to like a post. The heart turns red. Tap again to remove your like. The number next to the heart updates live. |
| 💬 **Comment** | Tap to expand the comment section. Type your comment and press **Send**. |
| 🔗 **Share** | Tap to share the post with your network. |
| ☰ **Menu** (three-dot icon) | Opens options: **Delete** *(only on your own posts)* and **Bookmark** |

**Voting on a poll:** Click any option in a poll card to cast your vote. Once you vote, the results are shown as percentage bars and you cannot change your vote. A *"✓ You voted"* label appears below the poll.

**Saving a post:** Open the three-dot menu on any post and choose **Bookmark**. The post is saved to your Saved Posts page. Choose **Bookmarked** again to remove it.

### 3.2 Jobs — Finding and Applying for Opportunities

The Jobs page lists all open positions posted by alumni, faculty, and administrators.

[INSERT SCREENSHOT HERE — Jobs page with the three tabs visible]

#### Browsing and Filtering Jobs

The page shows three tabs:

- **All Jobs** — every open listing on the platform
- **Applied** — applications you have submitted
- **My Jobs** — listings you have posted *(Alumni, Faculty, and Admin only)*

**Filtering the job list:**

At the top of the All Jobs tab, use the filter bar:

| Filter | How to use it |
|--------|--------------|
| **Search** | Type a job title, company name, or keyword |
| **Job Type** | Select: Full Time, Part Time, Contract, or Internship |
| **Location** | Type a city or location |

Click any job card to view the full description, requirements, and application details.

[INSERT SCREENSHOT HERE — A job listing card open with details and the Apply button]

#### Applying for a Job

1. Find a job you are interested in and open it.
2. Click **Apply Now**.
3. Fill in the application form:

| Field | What to Enter |
|-------|--------------|
| **Cover Letter** | Write a short message to the employer (up to 5,000 characters) |
| **Resume** | Upload your CV file |

4. Click **Submit Application**. Your submission will appear in the **Applied** tab.

> **Who can apply?** All logged-in members can apply for any job listing.

#### Posting a Job *(Alumni, Faculty, and Admin only)*

If you are an alumni, faculty member, or admin, a **Post a Job** button appears at the top of the Jobs page.

1. Click **Post a Job**.
2. Fill in the form:

| Field | Required | What to Enter |
|-------|----------|--------------|
| **Job Title** | yes | e.g. *Software Engineer Intern* |
| **Company** | yes | Name of the hiring organisation |
| **Location** | yes | City, country, or "Remote" |
| **Job Type** | yes | Full Time, Part Time, Contract, or Internship |
| **Description** | yes | Describe the role and responsibilities |
| **Requirements** | yes | List required qualifications, one per line |

3. Click **Create Job** to publish the listing. It will immediately appear under **All Jobs**.

### 3.3 Events — Attending and Hosting Events

The Events page is where you discover and register for department activities — from online webinars to in-person career fairs.

[INSERT SCREENSHOT HERE — Events page grid showing event cards]

#### Browsing Events

Events are displayed as cards in a grid. Each card shows the event name, type, date, and whether it is online or in-person.

The page has three tabs:

- **Upcoming** — all upcoming events (shows the total count)
- **My Events** — events you have created *(Faculty and Admin only)*
- **Attending** — events you have RSVPed to

#### RSVPing to an Event

1. Find an event you want to attend.
2. Click the **Attend** *(or similar)* button on the event card.
3. The event will be added to your **Attending** tab. Click the same button again to cancel your RSVP.

[INSERT SCREENSHOT HERE — Event card with the Attend button highlighted]

#### Creating an Event *(Faculty and Admin only)*

If you are a faculty member or admin, a **Create Event** button appears at the top of the page.

1. Click **Create Event**.
2. Fill in the form:

| Field | Required | What to Enter |
|-------|----------|--------------|
| **Event Title** | yes | The name of your event |
| **Event Type** | yes | Webinar, Workshop, Networking, Career Fair, or Seminar |
| **Description** | yes | What the event is about |
| **Cover Image** | no (optional) | Upload a banner image or paste an image link |
| **Start Date & Time** | yes | When the event begins |
| **End Date & Time** | yes | When the event ends |
| **Online Event** | Toggle | Switch on if the event is online |
| **Meeting Link** | (shown when online) | Paste the video call link (e.g. Zoom, Teams) |
| **Venue / Location** | (shown when in-person) | The physical address or room name |
| **Max Attendees** | (optional) | Leave blank for unlimited |

3. Click **Create Event** to publish.

[INSERT SCREENSHOT HERE — Create Event form dialog]

#### Deleting an Event *(Event creator only)*

On events you created, a 🗑 **Delete** button (red icon) appears in the top-right corner of the event card.

1. Click the delete icon.
2. A confirmation message appears: *"Are you sure you want to delete "[Event Name]"? This action cannot be undone."*
3. Click **Delete** to confirm, or **Cancel** to go back.

### 3.4 Research Hub — Exploring Academic Projects

The Research Hub is where faculty and students can browse ongoing academic research and join projects as collaborators.

[INSERT SCREENSHOT HERE — Research Hub page with project cards]

#### Browsing Research Projects

The page has three tabs:

- **All Projects** — every research project on the platform
- **My Research** — projects you are leading *(Faculty and Admin only)*
- **Collaborating** — projects you are participating in

Each project card shows the title, research field, status (Ongoing / Completed / Published), tags, and the lead researcher's name.

#### Joining a Project as a Collaborator

1. Find a project you are interested in.
2. Click the **Collaborate** button on the project card.
3. You will be added as a collaborator and the project will appear in your **Collaborating** tab.
4. To leave a project, click the **Leave** button on the same card.

[INSERT SCREENSHOT HERE — Research project card with Collaborate button]

#### Starting a New Research Project *(Faculty and Admin only)*

1. Click **New Project** at the top of the page.
2. Fill in the form:

| Field | Required | What to Enter |
|-------|----------|--------------|
| **Project Title** | yes | The name of the research project |
| **Description / Abstract** | yes | A summary of the research (minimum 10 characters) |
| **Research Field** | yes | e.g. Artificial Intelligence, Cybersecurity, Robotics |
| **Status** | yes | Ongoing, Completed, or Published |
| **Start Date** | yes | When the research began |
| **End Date** | (optional) | Expected completion date |
| **Funding Source** | (optional) | Name of the funding body, if any |
| **Cover Image** | (optional) | Upload a cover image or paste a URL |
| **Tags** | (optional) | Type a keyword and press **Enter** to add it (e.g. *Machine Learning*, *NLP*). Click the × on a tag to remove it. |

3. Click **Create Project** to publish.

[INSERT SCREENSHOT HERE — New Research Project form dialog]

#### Editing a Research Project *(Lead researcher only)*

As the lead researcher, an **Edit** button appears on your project cards.

1. Click **Edit** on your project.
2. Update any fields — title, description, status, research field, funding, tags.
3. Use the **Progress** slider to set the completion percentage (0% to 100%).
4. Click **Save Changes**.

### 3.5 Messages — Conversations and Group Chats

The Messages page lets you have private conversations with any platform member, or create group chats.

[INSERT SCREENSHOT HERE — Messages page showing the conversation list on the left and a chat open on the right]

#### Opening a Conversation

1. Click **Messages** in the sidebar.
2. Your existing conversations appear in the left panel. Conversations with unread messages show a green number badge.
3. Click a conversation to open it. Your messages appear on the right.

#### Searching Your Conversations

Type a name into the **Search Conversations** bar at the top of the conversation list to filter and find a specific chat.

#### Starting a New Direct Message

1. Click the **pencil icon** (✏️) at the top of the Messages panel.
2. A search box appears. Type the name of the person you want to message.
3. Click their name from the results.
4. A new conversation opens. Type your message and press **Enter** or click **Send**.

[INSERT SCREENSHOT HERE — New message dialog with user search]

#### Creating a Group Chat

1. Click the **group icon** (👥) at the top of the Messages panel.
2. Enter a **Group Name**.
3. Search for and select the members you want to add. Tick the checkbox next to each person's name.
4. Click **Create Group**.
5. The group chat opens immediately.

#### Sending a Message

- Type your message in the text field at the bottom of the chat (up to **2,000 characters**).
- Press **Enter** or click the **Send** button to send it.

The conversation list refreshes automatically every 10 seconds to show new incoming messages.

[INSERT SCREENSHOT HERE — Profile page showing the header, tabs, and About section]

| Field | What to Enter |

### 3.7 Connecting with Other Members

Connections are your network on the platform. When you connect with someone, you can more easily see their posts and interact with their profile.

#### Sending a Connection Request

1. Visit another member's profile by clicking their name or avatar anywhere on the platform (in a post, in search results, or in the Suggested Users widget on your feed).
2. Click the **Connect** button on their profile header.
3. They will receive a notification about your request.

[INSERT SCREENSHOT HERE — Another member's profile with the Connect button]

#### Accepting or Declining Requests

When someone sends you a connection request:

1. You will receive a notification (check the **Notifications** section or the Connections tab on your profile).
2. On your profile's **Connections** tab, pending requests appear with **Accept** and **Decline** buttons.
3. Click **Accept** to connect, or **Decline** to dismiss the request.

#### Suggested Connections

The right side of your Feed shows a **Suggested Users** widget with members you might want to connect with. Click **Connect** next to any suggestion to send a request directly from the feed.

### 3.8 Notifications — Staying Up to Date

The **Notifications** page shows all activity related to you — likes on your posts, new connection requests, event reminders, messages, and more.

[INSERT SCREENSHOT HERE — Notifications page with grouped notifications]

#### Reading Your Notifications

1. Click **Notifications** in the sidebar. A red badge shows how many unread notifications you have.
2. Notifications are organised into three groups:
   - **Today** — activity from the last 24 hours
   - **This week** — activity from the last 7 days
   - **Earlier** — older notifications

3. Each notification shows:
   - An icon indicating the type (like, comment, connection, job, event, message, research)
   - A short description (e.g. *"Alex liked your post"*)
   - A timestamp (e.g. *"3m ago"*)

4. Click a notification to go directly to the relevant content (e.g. the post, the message thread, the job listing).

#### Marking Notifications as Read

- **One at a time:** Clicking a notification automatically marks it as read.
- **All at once:** Click the **Mark all as read** button at the top of the page (only shown when you have unread notifications).

#### Deleting a Notification

Click the 🗑 **delete icon** on the right of any notification to remove it permanently.

## 5. Troubleshooting & FAQs

### Sign-In Problems

---

**"Invalid email or password" appears when I try to sign in.**

This message appears when the email address or password you entered does not match any account.

- Check that you typed your email address correctly — including the domain (e.g. `@gmail.com`).
- Passwords are case-sensitive. Make sure Caps Lock is off.
- If you have forgotten your password, click **Forgot Password?** on the login page and follow the reset steps.

The error banner dismisses itself after 5 seconds.

---

**I did not receive the password reset email.**

- Check your **Spam** or **Junk** folder.
- Make sure you entered the same email address you registered with.
- Wait up to 5 minutes and check again.
- If the problem persists, contact your platform administrator.

**"Login failed. Please try again." appears.**

This is a general error indicating a temporary problem connecting to the platform. Wait a few seconds and try again. If the issue continues, refresh the page.

---

### Registration Problems

---

**"Registration failed. Please try again."**

A temporary connection issue prevented your account from being created. Refresh the page and try registering again.

---

**"An account with this email already exists."**

The email address you entered is already linked to an existing account. Try signing in instead, or use the **Forgot Password** flow if you cannot remember your password.

---

**"Passwords do not match."**

The text in the **Password** and **Confirm Password** fields must be identical. Re-enter both fields carefully.

---

**"Password must be at least 6 characters."**

Your chosen password is too short. Use at least 6 characters. For a stronger password, include a mix of uppercase letters, numbers, and symbols.

---

**"Graduation year is required for students and alumni."**

If you chose the Student or Alumni role, the Graduation Year field on Step 3 is mandatory. Enter a year between 1980 and 2030.

---

**"First name must be at least 2 characters."** / **"Last name must be at least 2 characters."**

Names must have at least 2 characters. Single-letter names are not accepted.

---

### Posting Problems

---

**"Please enter some content or add media."**

You tried to submit a post without any text or attached files. Either type something in the text box, attach a photo or video, or create a poll before clicking Post.

---

**"Please add at least 2 poll options."**

If you clicked the poll button, you must provide at least 2 option texts before posting. Click the poll icon again to deactivate poll mode if you no longer need it.

---

**"Some files were too large. Max size is 20MB."**

One or more files you attached to the post exceed the 20 MB limit. Remove the oversized file and try again with a smaller version.

---

**"Failed to create post. Please try again."**

A temporary error occurred. Wait a moment and click **Post** again.

---

**"Content is too long."**

Post text is limited to **5,000 characters**. The character counter below the text box shows how many characters you have used. Shorten your text and try again.

---

**"Comment is required."** / **"Comment is too long."**

Comments must be between 1 and 1,000 characters. An empty comment cannot be submitted, and comments over 1,000 characters will be rejected.

---

### Profile & Settings Problems

---

**"Please select an image file."**

You tried to upload a non-image file as your profile picture. Only image formats (JPG, PNG, GIF, WebP) are accepted.

---

**"Image must be less than 5MB."**

The photo you chose exceeds the 5 MB limit for profile pictures. Resize or compress the image and try again.

---

**"Failed to upload image."**

A connection error occurred during the upload. Check your internet connection and try again.

---

**My profile changes are not showing.**

After saving your profile, try refreshing the page. If the changes still do not appear, log out and log back in.

---

### Jobs & Applications

---

**I cannot see the "Post a Job" button.**

Only **Alumni**, **Faculty**, and **Admin** members can post jobs. If you are a Student, this feature is not available to you. If you believe your role is set incorrectly, contact the platform administrator.

---

**"Description must be at least 10 characters."** / **"Title is required."** / **"Company is required."**

These fields in the job creation form cannot be left blank or too short. Fill them in completely before clicking **Create Job**.

---

### Events & Research

---

**I cannot see the "Create Event" button.**

Creating events is limited to **Faculty** and **Admin** members. Students and alumni can browse and RSVP to events but cannot create them.

---

**I cannot see the "New Project" button in the Research Hub.**

Starting new research projects is limited to **Faculty** and **Admin** members. Students and alumni can browse projects and join as collaborators.

---

**"Title is required."** / **"Description must be at least 10 characters."** (in event or research forms)**

These fields are mandatory. Fill them in before submitting the form.

---

### Messages

---

**"Message is required."**

You tried to send an empty message. Type something in the message box before pressing Send.

---

**"Message is too long."**

Messages are limited to **2,000 characters**. Shorten your message and try again.

---

**My conversation list is not updating.**

The messages page refreshes your conversation list every **10 seconds** automatically. If a new conversation is not appearing, wait a few seconds or try refreshing the page manually.

---

### General

---

**The page is blank or not loading.**

1. Refresh your browser.
2. Check that you are connected to the internet.
3. Try logging out and logging back in.
4. If the problem persists, clear your browser cache and cookies.

---

**I want to report inappropriate content.**

Use the three-dot menu (⋮) on any post to find the available options. For serious concerns, contact the platform administrator directly.
